01 — Context

A single screen was silently killing conversions in every new market.

Gymondo had recently expanded to English-speaking markets where the brand was completely unknown. Conversion rates were disappointing — and data was pointing at one clear culprit.

The Go Premium screen, shown during onboarding, had a massive drop-off rate. Users were hitting a paywall before they'd experienced any value. At the same time, the subscription model itself was outdated compared to competitors, with no trial or freemium entry point.

02 — Research

The real fix wasn't the screen — it was where it lived.

We benchmarked multiple apps with similar subscription models — primarily fitness competitors, but also other app categories to explore layout options.

The key insight: removing the screen from onboarding entirely would let users experience the product before being asked to pay. Combined with a freemium content strategy, this could fundamentally change the conversion dynamic.

01
Rethink placement
Move Go Premium out of onboarding — let users reach the main app screen before encountering a paywall.
02
Simplify the screen
Reduce information density, give prominence to Gymondo's value proposition so it resonates in new markets.

Smart free content
Strategically gate content so users get a taste, then feel motivated to unlock the rest by converting.

03 — UX Design

Shorter onboarding. Smarter free content. Better timing.

Removing Go Premium from onboarding shortened the flow considerably — users reached the main screen faster and with less friction. The paywall moved to a contextual moment: when a user tried to unlock premium content they'd already engaged with.

This shift from "pay to enter" to "pay to unlock more" fundamentally changed the conversion psychology.

New user flow diagram
04 — UX Audit

The existing screen had more problems than we expected.

An audit of the original Go Premium screen surfaced several compounding issues: fake-looking review badges undermining trust, subscription packages re-appearing confusingly at the bottom, unclear highlighted vs. selected states, and too much scrolling required to even understand what Gymondo offered.

Each of these compounded the core problem. The redesign had to fix them all simultaneously.

"Too much scroll to explain what Gymondo offers — users in new markets were lost before they even considered paying."

05 — Testing

Two proposals. Two weeks. One clear winner.

We built two variants to test against the existing screen. The reason for two proposals was to check whether users responded better to a video-led approach (Variant B — less text, more visual) vs. a copy-led approach (Variant A — more explanation of Gymondo's value).

The A/B test ran for two weeks. Variant B won — even though Variant A was closing the gap by the end, Variant B consistently converted better throughout the entire test period.

A

Variant B — Visual-led 🏆

06 — Results

Significant impact on the bottom line — in every market.

The winning screen shipped as a modular component, designed for easy iteration. Future improvements can be made directly in-app with a permanent A/B test loop — no full redesign needed.
