01 — Context

More than half of new users were leaving before they ever saw what NeoTaste could do.

NeoTaste is a restaurant discovery and deals platform used by hundreds of thousands of food lovers across Germany. Users sign up to explore local restaurants, unlock exclusive deals, and book tables directly in the app.

When I joined the Growth team in early 2024, the data told a clear story: 54% of newly registered users dropped off during onboarding — never reaching the home feed and therefore never experiencing the product's core value.

The existing flow had grown organically over 3 years. What started as a simple 3-step setup had ballooned into a 7-screen sequence with permission requests, lengthy form fields, and marketing opt-ins — all before showing the user a single restaurant.

Old onboarding — 7 steps
02 — Research

Session recordings and eight user interviews surfaced the same friction points again and again.

Before touching a wireframe, I spent two weeks in discovery. I reviewed session recordings in Hotjar, analysed the step-by-step funnel in Mixpanel, and ran eight 45-minute moderated interviews with recently churned users recruited through a post-signup survey.

The funnel data confirmed that step 3 (location permissions) and step 5 (notification opt-in) had the highest abandonment. The interviews told us why: users felt the app was asking for too much before proving its worth.

"I didn't understand why it needed my location before I'd even seen what the app looked like. I just closed it."

03 — Process

From 7 friction-heavy steps to 3 — with value shown before anything is asked.

The guiding principle became: earn the right to ask. Show the product first. Let users experience the core value — browsing nearby restaurants and deals — before requesting permissions or preferences.

I mapped three concept directions, workshopped them with the PM and engineering lead, and moved into prototype testing within two weeks. Each concept was tested with 6 participants in unmoderated sessions via Maze.

04 — Solution

A value-first experience that earns trust before asking for anything.

The redesigned onboarding opens with an animated restaurant card carousel — real venues from the user's region, showing deal prices and cuisine types. The first 15 seconds are spent watching the product work, not filling out a form.

Account creation was stripped to the minimum: email and password, plus Apple/Google sign-in. Dietary preferences were removed entirely from onboarding and replaced by a progressive profiling system that learns from saved items and search behaviour over the first week.

Permission asks were deferred to context: location is requested the first time a user searches, push notifications are requested the first time a user saves a deal. Both convert at over 60% in this contextual state vs. under 30% in the old flow.

01
Value-first splash screen
Full-screen animated cards show real deals from local restaurants. Users see the product before they commit. Avg. time on this screen: 18 seconds.
02
Minimal account creation
Email + password, or one-tap Apple/Google sign-in. No address, no preferences, no consent walls. Time to complete: under 40 seconds.
03
Immediate home feed access
Users land in the full product experience. A subtle first-use tooltip sequence guides discovery of the three core actions over the first session.
04
Contextual permission system
Location and notification permissions are requested at the exact moment they add value — during first search and first deal save respectively.
05 — Results

Drop-off cut in half. Day-1 activation nearly doubled.

The redesigned flow shipped to 100% of new users in Q3 2024 after a successful 2-week A/B test at 20% traffic. Results were measured across a 30-day post-launch window.
