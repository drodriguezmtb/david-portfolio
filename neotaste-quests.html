01 — Context

New users were signing up, completing onboarding — and then disappearing within 72 hours.

With 2 million foodies on the platform, NeoTaste had cracked acquisition. But cohort data revealed a concerning pattern: 38% of users who completed onboarding became inactive within their first 3 days, never returning after their initial session.

The app is rich with features — map discovery, exclusive 2-for-1 deals, free desserts, table bookings, neighbourhood guides — but new users only scratched the surface. They'd browse the home feed once, save nothing, book nothing, and lose interest before experiencing NeoTaste's core value loop.

The challenge wasn't onboarding completion. It was habit formation. How do you make a new user go from "this looks interesting" to "I use this every week"?

02 — Research

The data showed a clear cliff at Day 3. Users who made a booking in the first week never left.

I partnered with our Data Analyst to run a behavioural segmentation across 6 months of cohort data. The finding was striking: users who completed at least two distinct actions in their first 48 hours had a D30 retention rate of 71% — versus 12% for those who only viewed the feed.

The "magic actions" were saving a restaurant, redeeming a deal, and making a booking. Users who did all three in week one became power users. The problem: only 8% of new users discovered all three naturally.

"I completely forgot it was on my phone. I never actually used a deal — I just assumed I'd get around to it."

03 — Design

Five quests. Forty-eight hours. Designed to make every new user feel like a power user.

The Quest system presents new users with a set of guided missions that expire after 48 hours from sign-up. Each quest teaches one core app behaviour through direct action — not tutorial text or tooltips. Completing a quest earns a visible reward and unlocks the next mission.

The design deliberately avoided the feel of a tutorial. Quests are presented as personal challenges, framed around the user's own culinary exploration — not as app education. The progress tracker lives as a persistent but non-intrusive card on the home feed.

04 — Results

Day-7 retention improved by a third. Feature adoption more than doubled.

The Quest system launched in Q4 2024 after a 3-week A/B test on 25% of new user traffic. Results were measured against the control group (no quests) over a 60-day post-signup window.

The most significant finding: quest completers had a D30 retention rate of 68% — nearly matching the historic power user segment that we'd benchmarked the system against.
