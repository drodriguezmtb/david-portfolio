01 — Context

Spain's largest bank had a mobile app that no longer reflected who they were.

CaixaBank needed to replace an ageing app used by millions of customers across Spain. The old product had accumulated years of feature debt, inconsistent UI patterns, and no unified design language.

VASS brought me in as a consultant to work in-house from CaixaBank's offices in Barcelona, embedded within their product team. The brief was ambitious: design a new iOS and Android app from scratch, and simultaneously establish a design system that would scale to the web and ATM network.

Hero app screens — replace with Figma exports
02 — Approach

Design system first. Screens second.

Before drawing a single screen, we needed a shared language. I led the creation of a component library that would serve as the single source of truth for app, web, and ATM interfaces.

Working closely with CaixaBank's development teams and other designers in the consultancy, we defined tokens, components, and patterns from the ground up — ensuring every touchpoint felt cohesive.

This foundation let us move fast on the app itself, iterating on flows and layouts without rebuilding basics each time.

03 — Output

A cohesive product family across app, web, and ATM.

The new app launched to millions of CaixaBank customers, replacing the legacy product entirely. The design system ensured that every touchpoint — mobile, desktop, and in-branch ATMs — spoke the same visual language.

The industry response was immediate. In 2018, the project won four international awards — recognition from Global Finance, The Banker, BAI, and the London Design Awards.
