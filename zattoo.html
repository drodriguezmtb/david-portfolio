01 — Context

Two Android TV teams. Two products. One designer to bridge them.

Zattoo is a live TV streaming platform offering access to hundreds of channels across Europe — available on smart TVs, set-top boxes, mobile, and web. When I joined, the design team needed someone to lead across two distinct Android TV teams simultaneously.

The challenge was managing D2C (direct-to-consumer) and B2B (white-label for operators) products that shared architecture but served very different user expectations — all while a significant platform migration from native Android to React was underway.

03 — D2C Product

Consumer TV: familiar, fast, and made for the couch.

The D2C product serves everyday TV watchers who want live channels, catch-up content, and recordings — all in one place. Design decisions needed to feel instantly intuitive on a 10-foot screen, navigated entirely with a remote.

Key focus areas included improving content discovery, refining the EPG (Electronic Programme Guide) interaction model, and ensuring the UI could accommodate both lean-back browsing and active searching — two very different modes of use.

D2C consumer TV UI
04 — B2B Product

White-label TV: flexible enough for operators, consistent enough to trust.

B2B white-label operator product

The B2B product serves telecoms operators who white-label Zattoo's platform for their own subscriber base. This meant designing components flexible enough to accommodate operator branding and content variation while maintaining a coherent user experience.

Close collaboration with the engineering team was essential — understanding where theming was possible in the React architecture directly informed which design decisions were viable at scale.

05 — Platform Migration

Native to React — keeping the experience intact through the transition.

Migrating a live TV product from native Android to React is a significant engineering undertaking — and one that carries real risk of design regression. Animations feel different. Layouts can shift. Timing changes.

I worked closely with engineers throughout the migration to identify and resolve design inconsistencies before they reached users, and contributed updated component specs to the design system to reflect the new platform constraints and capabilities.

"The migration wasn't just a tech project — it was a design quality project. Every screen was an opportunity to improve, not just replicate."
